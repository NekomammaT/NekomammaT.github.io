The 14th International Conference on Gravitation, Astrophysics and Cosmology (ICGAC14)
Manifestly covariant theory of stochastic inflation ▼
The stochastic approach to inflation can non-perturbatively treat the superhorizon fluctuations as the classical random noise to go beyond the standard perturbative QFT approach. I first review how this approach enables us to calculate the observable curvature perturbations in a non-perturbative way and how it can be implemented in a numerical program. I then describe the covariance of the stochastic theory and the discretization scheme of the stochastic noise, which are important both theoretically and practically. Mathematically the stochastic noise can be discretized in an arbitrary manner, but we reveal the physically motivated discretization, which ensures the covariance of the stochastic theory on the inflatons' target manifold.
L. Pinol, S. Renaux-Petel, Y. Tada, V. Vennin
oral @ National Central University, Taiwan (Web Conference)

Escape from the swamp with spectator ▼
近年弦理論の文脈で、ある種の低エネルギー有効理論がUV完全でない可能性を指摘する沼地予想が注目され、UV完全な有効理論が満たすべき様々な仮説的条件が提唱されている。これらはインフレーション機構にも制限を与え、典型的には長く続く加速膨張期を禁じる傾向がある。そこで我々は短いインフレーションが多数繰り返され合わせて必要な膨張量を達成する模型に着目し、これらが実際沼地仮説の条件を満たすことを示した。またこの模型のもと、大スケールでほぼスケール不変な初期ゆらぎをスペクテイター場によって実現し得ることも議論する。

About Me

I'm a theoretical scientist in Nagoya university, Japan. My main interest is physics of the early stage of our universe. In particular, I have so far focused on inflation, the accelerated expansion preceding the hot Big-Bang universe. I have proposed a novel analysing approach to inflation with use of mathematics of random noise called stochastic calculus. Primordial black hole is also my research topic as a direct consequence of particular dynamics of the early universe. I'm also working on several production mechanism of gravitational waves. I'm from Saitama, Japan. I completed a B.Sc. in Physics in 2012 and a M.Sc. in Physics in 2014, and received a Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Tokyo in March 2017.
